TWO — AI Scaffold Studio

27–55 min

Build one AI scaffold for your student from ONE, test it against use boundaries, then run a risk check.

AI Scaffold Studio ~9 min

Reference — Maureen's five use-cases:

  • Planning — "Turn this assignment into a checklist. Do not do the work for me."
  • Reading — "Identify the main argument, key terms, and questions to guide my reading."
  • Writing initiation — "Ask me one question at a time to help me clarify my argument. Do not write the essay."
  • Feedback — "Tell me whether my evidence supports my claim. Do not rewrite it for me."
  • College access — "Explain these steps in plain language and make a checklist. Tell me what information I should not enter."

Green / Yellow / Red AI Use Builder ~8 min

Green — encouragedClarify instructions, brainstorm, build a study plan, explain concepts, support accessibility.
Yellow — disclose / reflectFeedback on drafts, outlines, grammar or clarity support, revision suggestions.
"I used AI to ___. I accepted ___. I rejected ___. The final thinking is mine because ___."
Red — not allowedSubmit generated work as one's own, fabricate sources, bypass the learning objective, enter sensitive or private data.

Risk and Agency Check ~6 min

Fast pass — yes or revise, for each:

PrivacyDisclosureVoiceOverreliance IntegrityAccessHuman loopChallenge
6:00

AI is one tool on a bigger shelf.

Everything you just tiered into green, yellow, and red — that same test applies to every assistive tool a student touches, not just AI. A support is helpful if it reduces load, increases choice, builds skill, can be used without shame, and avoids unnecessary data collection. It needs revision if it adds steps, creates surveillance, replaces the learning goal, requires disclosure, or overwhelms the student.
